## Overview

Point-of-care transthoracic echocardiography (TTE) is a low-cost, non-invasive tool for diagnosing 
common cardiac diseases, but acquiring and interpreting TTE images requires specialized training, 
limiting patient access. While extensive prior work focuses on TTE interpretation, the equally 
critical problem of image acquisition remains largely unaddressed. Here, we introduce a novel 
method for flow-based ultrasound guidance that enables even untrained operators to acquire 
diagnostically valuable TTE imaging studies. Specifically, we formulate the task of ultrasound 
transducer placement as a distribution mapping problem over the space of $SE(3)$ poses. Using flow 
matching, we train a network to learn a velocity field transporting from the distribution of 
arbitrary poses to that of canonical view poses. Sampling from this field constitutes a 
closed-loop process: conditioned on the current image and desired view class, the network predicts 
a velocity in $se(3)$, which is conveyed to the operator as real-time probe adjustment instructions 
via a graphical interface. As in generative image models, we find in simulation that classifier-free 
guidance yields improvements in convergence and pose alignment. Using this approach, we collected 1 
hour of training data, consisting of ultrasound images and tracked transducer poses, on a single 
subject and evaluated performance on 3 unseen subjects. In qualitative and quantitative comparisons 
with 5 novice and intermediate operators, we found that the addition of model-based guidance 
significantly decreased acquisition time for the 6 most common TTE views. These findings indicate 
that flow-based guidance models may substantially close the performance gap between novice and 
trained sonographers, expanding access to point-of-care cardiac imaging in resource-limited settings.
