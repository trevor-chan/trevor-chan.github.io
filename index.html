About me

Trevor Chan is an engineer and designer from Oakland, CA. He graduated Yale University in 2020, where he obtained degrees in mechanical engineering and architecture. Currently, he works at the Mak Research Lab at Yale, where he investigates cancer cell mechanobiology.

He has worked on numerous projects, spanning a wide range of disciplines. These include planning an elementary school renovation focused on energy sustainability and resilience for the U.S. Department of Energy Solar Decathlon Challenge, collaborating with orthopedic surgeons in the Yale Medical School to design an original surgical retractor for foot and ankle surgery, and employing AI to segment brightfield images and characterize mechanical behaviors of cancer cell lineages.
